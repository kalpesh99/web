India’s Dysfunction Is Not an Accident — It’s the Product of Incompetence, Dishonesty, and Apathy

This is not academic theory. It’s a practical diagnosis of why the Indian state underperforms and what harsh, outcome-driven fixes would be required to reverse it. If you’re looking for polite phrasing or comforting narratives, this page is not for you.

Core thesis: demand competence + honesty Benchmark: China / Japan / Korea Method: accountability + enforcement

Note: This reflects one researcher’s observations and proposed fixes. It intentionally avoids sugar-coating.

Premise

India doesn’t fail because it lacks talent, resources, or “potential.” It fails because the system tolerates — and often rewards — incompetence and dishonesty.

Most reforms fail because they try to fix symptoms (a program, a scheme, a committee) while keeping the root cause intact: no real accountability, no fear of consequences, and a voting public that doesn’t consistently demand competence and honesty.

1) People

Problems
  • Premature democracy for an ignorant population that doesn’t recognize or value democracy.
  • Upper class (leaders, officers, businessmen, educated, middle class) corrupted by money/power; brainwashed/controlled by propaganda (“India shining”), fake pride, nationalism, religion, superiority, lack of critical thinking.
  • Lower class (workers, low-skill, farmers) lacks education, skills, critical thinking; disillusioned; easily swayed by freebies, caste/tribe/religion politics, reservations. Survival mode dominates.
  • People don’t demand competence and honesty — the two most important traits for a successful state.
Fix
  • Education (but not the current memorization factory; see Education section below).
  • Culture shift: competence + honesty must become non-negotiable voting criteria, not optional “nice-to-haves.”

If voters reward theatrics and identity over outcomes, governance will stay theatrical and identity-driven.

2) Leaders

Problem

Not demanding competence and honesty produces predictable results: no rule of law, institutionalized corruption, and leaders focused on power and money instead of people.

Fix
  • People MUST NOT elect incompetent and dishonest leaders.
  • Stop normalizing “lesser evil” voting. It guarantees long-term rot.

3) Government Employees

Incompetence

Problems
  • Highly educated on paper, but useless in real life outcomes.
  • Incentives reward seniority, connections, and safe mediocrity.
  • Little to no measurable accountability for ground-level performance.
Fixes (hard, outcome-based)
  • Competition among peers: each department should have 3 leaders competing on concrete metrics.
  • Benchmark against China/Japan/Korea (not internal comparisons).
  • Copy successful: use China as benchmark; copy-paste models that work.
  • Remove SC/ST/OBC/EWS reservation and DEI policies.
  • Hire only competent people; fire incompetence fast.
  • Continuously compare performance to China/Japan/Korea; remove bottom performers.

Dishonesty

Problem

Institutionalized corruption — dishonesty isn’t an exception; it’s a workflow.

Fixes
  • Gulag for dishonest officers.
  • Remove institutionalized dishonesty (systems that force bribery as the default).
  • 10% monetary reward for exposing corruption.

4) Rule of Law

Without rule of law, everything becomes negotiable: contracts, safety, property rights, dignity, justice. A state without rule of law is not a “democracy”; it’s a marketplace of influence.

Judiciary

Problem

Dishonest and incompetent leadership; cases pile up; consequences don’t land.

Fixes
  • Gulag bottom 25% dishonest judges in year one; 50% in two years.
  • AI-based case evaluation and judgment.
  • Two years to clear most pending cases.

Police

Problem

Low accountability, high discretion, incentives to serve power rather than public safety.

Fixes
  • Gulag bottom 25% dishonest police (especially leaders) in year one; 50% in two years.
  • 80% police on bikes with gun, camera, and phone connected to control tower.
  • Case filing + citizen feedback + brutality/competence/honesty rating through PMO hotline.
  • Let go of bottom performers.

Government employees (again)

Fix

Gulag for government employee incompetence/dishonesty.

Citizens

Problem

Fraud and predation become normal when enforcement is weak and courts are slow.

Fixes
  • Gulag for fraudsters and habitual criminals.
  • Caning for extreme criminals (rapists, etc.).

A society that refuses to punish bad actors quickly and consistently eventually becomes a society run by bad actors.

5) Education

Problems
  • Private, money-motivated system with little focus on skills, behavior, innovation, critical thinking.
  • DI leadership (dishonest + incompetent leadership).
  • Memorization rewarded; curiosity punished.
Fixes
  • AI-based education to study academics at home.
  • Use school teachers for skills, behavior, innovation instead of rote content delivery.
  • Morale: teach values, history, and “right things to do.”
  • Focus on critical thinking, skills, behavior, morale, innovation over memorization.

6) Healthcare

Problems
  • Money-driven, cheating-heavy system.
  • State hospitals: poor infrastructure and service quality.
  • DI leadership.
Fixes
  • CC leadership: single point of contact accountable for outcomes.
  • WhatsApp/video/AI app for free consultation.
  • Public health centers with competent doctors for nominal fees.
  • Able administrator with independent feedback through PMO hotline.
  • Strict enforcement of food, drug, water, air safety.

7) Cities

Problems
  • Dirty
  • No planning
  • Aesthetically bad
  • No traffic management
  • DI leadership
Fixes
  • CC leadership: single accountable leader.
  • No animals on streets. Catch and send to camps.
  • No hawkers on streets. Move into designated hawker markets (Singapore-style).
  • No encroachment on footpaths by shops. Enforce via private party.
  • One lane for parking/stopover; clear lane marking.
  • Camera-based enforcement; ID-linked demerit points.
  • Clean streets; no garbage.
  • No telecom wires hanging; only power wires overhead.
  • Use Chinese city planning as template: choose from a catalog and blindly copy.
  • Citizen app feedback with picture/video.

8) Industry

Problems
  • Low manufacturing base vs China/Japan/Korea due to cost, regulation, incompetence, corruption.
  • Heavy dependence on outsourced IT services.
  • High youth unemployment.
Fixes
  • Hire five salespeople to sell India as a manufacturing base.
  • Offer free land, building, infrastructure (electricity, transport hookup).
  • Create clusters by industry.
  • Cheap infrastructure benchmarked against China.

9) Infrastructure

Problems
  • Poor quality due to corruption.
  • Private companies owning and charging high.
  • DI leadership.
Fixes
  • CC leadership with accountability.
  • Infrastructure should be public-owned where there are fewer than 3 direct competitors.
  • Public: water, wastewater, electricity, road, rail, airport, seaport.
  • Requests for infrastructure from cities/industry go to PMO → infra department.

10) Feedback Loop

Problem

Leaders don’t know what’s happening at street level (and often don’t want to know).

Fix
  • Create a separate citizen-to-PMO feedback channel for each service/department.
  • Make feedback actionable with evidence (photo/video), time-bound responses, and public dashboards.

If you can’t measure it, you can’t fix it. If you can measure it but nobody is punished for failure, you still can’t fix it.

Closing

India’s core problem is not “policy.” It’s selection + incentives + enforcement. A system that doesn’t punish dishonesty and incompetence rapidly will be run by dishonest and incompetent people.

The minimum viable national standard should be simple: competence and honesty are the entry ticket. Everything else comes after.
